# Recently Deleted Packages

The Recently Deleted Packages page acts as a recycle bin for your deleted packages, giving you the opportunity to restore or permanently delete them before they are automatically removed. This is useful in cases of accidental deletion.

Recently Deleted Packages is available at both the workspace and repository level. The workspace-level view aggregates deleted packages across all repositories, while the repository-level view is scoped to a single repository.

<BlockImage src={recentlyDeletedPage} alt="Recently deleted packages page"></BlockImage>

## Package retention

Deleted packages are retained for 7 days from the time of deletion, after which they are permanently and automatically removed.

## Restore packages

To restore an individual package, click the arrow icon on the right-hand side of the table row. To restore multiple packages at once, select the packages you want to restore, then choose **Restore** from the actions menu at the bottom of the page.

Packages will be restored to their original repository.

<BlockImage src={restorePackages} alt="Restore packages"></BlockImage>

## Permanently delete packages

To permanently delete an individual package, click the trash icon on the right-hand side of the table row. To permanently delete multiple packages at once, select the packages you want to remove, then choose **Permanently delete** from the actions menu at the bottom of the page.

<Note variant="warning">
  Once permanently deleted, packages can no longer be restored.
</Note>

<BlockImage src={deletePackages} alt="Delete packages"></BlockImage>

## Deleted repositories

When a repository is deleted, all of its packages are transferred to Recently Deleted Packages. If you restore a package from a deleted repository, the repository will be automatically restored as well.
